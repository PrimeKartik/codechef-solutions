## 📚 Key Learnings

**1. Equal Coins**
* When distributing values, checking parity (even/odd) is the first step, but always account for the "zero-availability" edge case (like having no 1-rupee coins to split an odd number of 2-rupee coins).

**2. Caesar Cipher**
* When calculating circular differences between characters, adding the alphabet size (26) before the modulo operation `(diff + 26) % 26` is the definitive way to prevent negative wrap-around bugs.

**3. Chef-jumping**
* Infinite sequence simulations can be compressed into `O(1)` time complexity by mapping the cumulative distance of one full cycle and testing against its modulo.
