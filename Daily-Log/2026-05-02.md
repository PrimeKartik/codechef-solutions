## 📚 Key Learnings

**1. Racing Horses**
* Sorting an array reduces the search for the minimum absolute difference from `O(N^2)` to `O(N log N)` because the closest pairs will always end up adjacent to each other.

**2. A Good Set**
* A sequence of purely odd numbers naturally satisfies the condition `a + b != c` because the sum of two odd numbers always results in an even number.

**3. Truth and Dare**
* Using auxiliary arrays to map the availability of states (like tasks) enables `O(1)` lookup times when validating against a requested subset, completely avoiding nested loops.
